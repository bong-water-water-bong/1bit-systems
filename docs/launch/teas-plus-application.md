## Filing mechanics

1. Go to https://teas.uspto.gov/forms/us1050
2. Pick **TEAS Plus** (not TEAS Standard; the $250 flat rate requires Plus)
3. Accept the TEAS Plus rules (ID Manual descriptions only, signed declaration, all correspondence electronic)
4. For each filing, the rough form sections:
   - Applicant Information → your info
   - Mark Information → "1BIT.SYSTEMS" etc as plain text
   - Classes/Goods/Services → pick the pre-populated descriptions above (they match the ID Manual already)
   - Specimens → upload PNG/PDF per filing
   - Signature → your full legal name, "applicant" as title
5. Pay by credit card per filing ($250 × 4 = $1,000)
6. You receive a **serial number** per filing within minutes. Save all four.

## After filing

- Expect initial examining-attorney review in ~3 months
- Office Actions (if any) get emailed to `d1r7yman@gmail.com`; most Office Actions are minor (specimen quibbles, ID clarifications)
- Publication for opposition happens ~6-8 months in
- Registration (assuming no opposition) ~12-14 months in
- Maintenance: first filing at 5 years, renewal every 10 years

## Things NOT to do

- Don't file via a trademark "service" like LegalZoom or TrademarkEngine — they charge 3-10× USPTO fees for paperwork you can do yourself
- Don't skip the specimen. The mark must be shown IN USE in the marketplace. Screenshot of 1bit.systems already counts as "use."
- Don't file in the International/Madrid system yet. Start domestic, expand later if international enforcement becomes necessary.
- Don't file additional classes beyond what you actually use. Each extra class is $250 and triggers new examination.
