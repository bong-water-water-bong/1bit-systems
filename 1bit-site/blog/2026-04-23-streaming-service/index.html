Presenting

1bit.systems — streaming audio and video

Every catalog in 2 MB on the free tier. Bit-perfect on Premium. Audio and video. Decoded locally on your gfx1151 iGPU, not on our servers.

2026-04-23 · product announcement · all launch timelines gated on training completion

What it is

A streaming service where the catalog fits in your pocket. Specifically: the free tier of a given artist's discography is smaller than one song at 320 kbps MP3. The tradeoff is the tradeoff you already knew about — compression at this ratio is lossy; the output is neurally generated rather than a bit-for-bit re-read of a master tape. Premium tier gives you bit-exact decode of the original source at about 30% less disk than FLAC.

Music tier first, then video.

Two tiers, one runtime

Free — 4 000× smaller than FLAC

A 1.58-bit ternary language model memorises an artist's audio codec tokens. Prompt the model with a track name and it emits Mimi codec tokens; the Mimi decoder runs them through a waveform synthesiser. The whole pack for one artist's canon fits in around 2 MB.

  • Recognisable, listenable, not bit-exact.
  • MOS target: 3.8 / 5 (comparable to a low-bitrate Opus stream, perceptually).
  • Instant load, no network round-trip per track.

Premium — lossless, ~30% below FLAC

Same 1.58-bit LM, but now it serves as the probability model inside an arithmetic coder over the original FLAC bytes. Decoder has the same model + same context; arithmetic-decode is byte-exact. File size sits near the Shannon lower bound for music content (~15–20% below FLAC), with small fixed overhead for the LM weights.

  • Checksummable against the original master.
  • ~6 GB for a catalog that takes 8 GB in FLAC.
  • Real-time decode on a gfx1151 iGPU via our HIP ternary kernel.

Why this can exist

The two pieces have to come together in one place. That place is halo-ai. We built the ternary GEMV kernel (92% of LPDDR5X peak on Strix Halo), we built the iGPU-native ggml backend, we build the training pipeline on a 2× H200 NVL RunPod block, and the payment rails are already up (BTCPay Lightning on sliger, Patreon through our MCP). The last missing piece is the catalog format and the storefront.

The CLI already exists in spirit:

1bit install kevin-macleod-catalog     # pulls 2 MB .gguf, you can play offline
1bit install kevin-macleod-lossless    # adds the ~6 GB residual, tier upgrade

No Beatles button. The lossy 4 000× pitch works for copyrighted catalogs only as a technical demo; we do not ship reconstructions of masters we don't have the right to reproduce. If you want your own copyrighted library in .1bl format, the encoder ships in the 1bit desktop client and runs locally on your machine. Your files stay on your box.

Video — same trick, different kernel

Wan 2.2 TI2V-5B is the video-side counterpart. Same pipeline: ternary-weight LM learns a codec-token stream (video tokens instead of audio), lossy reconstruction on the free tier, lossless arithmetic-coded residual on Premium. Video lane is gated on our Wan 2.2 5D patch-embedding loader landing in the 1bit-ggml fork (pull request open) and the ternary DiT retrain queued behind audio work.

Price

Free tier is free. Premium pricing not set; reference point is the ~$10/mo tier on existing lossless streaming services. We intend to undercut because our cost basis is decode-on-client rather than transcode-at-server. Draft: $5/mo or $50/yr, BTCPay Lightning or Patreon. No ad integration. No tracking. No listening telemetry.

What's blocking the launch

  1. Run 5 (current Sparse-BitNet LLM retrain) finishing on the pod. Training pod is serialised.
  2. Run 6 (ternary Qwen3-TTS) — unlocks the lossy-audio training recipe.
  3. Run 7 (ternary Wan 2.2) — unlocks the video tier.
  4. Run 8 (1bit-ac generalist lossless compressor) — unlocks Premium. Details.
  5. Catalog onboarding CLI — artist-facing tool to encode a corpus → publish-ready pack.
  6. USPTO TEAS Plus trademark filings on 1bit.systems and 1bit-ac. Sub-$500, same week.

Numbered order is the honest order. We are not pre-selling subscriptions. This is an announcement of intent and a public commit to the architecture; when the runs finish and the code ships, the storefront flips on.

Honest caveats

  • The 4 000× number is lossy. Audiophiles listen on Premium.
  • The 30% lossless gain is a Shannon-bound result; competitors with equally-good neural entropy coders will land in the same neighbourhood. Our moat is iGPU-native real-time decode, not the ratio itself.
  • Launch timelines are training-dependent. Training is cheap but serial on one pod.
  • We are not replacing Spotify. We are showing what happens when every part of the stack runs on a 128 GB mini-PC under your desk.
