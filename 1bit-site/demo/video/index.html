The first vampire film, and the film whose copyright predates most currently-active copyright law in the United States. An unauthorised Dracula adaptation that Bram Stoker's widow tried to burn into oblivion and still exists because celluloid survives. German Expressionism's clearest surviving artefact.

Night of the Living Dead

1968 · George A. Romero · 96 min

In the public domain because the original 1968 theatrical prints were released without a copyright notice — a pre-1976 formal requirement. Romero never recovered the commercial rights and the film became free for distribution. Founded the modern zombie genre; shot for $114 000; still in every film-school syllabus.

Pre-committed

The Great Dictator (1940)

Chaplin's anti-fascist comedy enters the US public domain on January 1, 2036 (95-year term from publication). On day one of its public-domain status we ingest, train a dedicated ternary DiT, and ship both tiers in the same week. Queued in memory, calendar reminder set. Playing the long game.

Modern Times (1936) · City Lights (1931)

Both Chaplin films enter public domain in 2032 and 2027 respectively. Same pre-commitment: first-day ingest, same-week publish.

Same architecture, different kernel

The lossy free tier uses a ternary DiT (10–40 M parameters depending on video resolution) trained to memorise the film's Wan 2.2 token stream. The lossless Premium tier is the same DiT as the probability model inside an arithmetic coder over the original MP4 bytestream — identical sha256 back to the source. See the lossless audio write-up; video uses the same mechanism on a different codec.
